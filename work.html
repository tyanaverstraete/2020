HUMAN BODY & WATER
2020

Assignment: own project
Theme: human body & water
Material: glass
Size: various
(still in process)

These forms made out of glass refer to images I recall from how I see body parts under water.
The movements are so quick and not always completly recognizable. The glass figures are in blance
of their ability to float.

Souvenir
2019

Assignment: collaboration project
Theme: recycling
Material: glass
Size: 52 sticks each 15cm long and a box 18cmx18cm

A project where a place in Israel has been visualised.
It is a game made out of recycled material, given color according to the RGB-system.
Each stick is unique and refers to a picture from a bigger entity: the district
Nachlaot in Jerusalem.






ACCUMULATION I
2019

Assignment: further interpretation on project groentopia
Theme: fragility of the spine
Material: glass, porcelain
Size: depends of the construction, but one piece is around 5cmx5cm

Accumulation I refers to the possible flaws within the spine.
The composition seeks the boundary between balance and tension.
This is due to the fragile choice of materials, and by placing
the forms too close so that they can collapse at any moment.

GROENTOPIA
2018

Assignment: interpretation to an artist of choice
Theme: materiality
Material: studies in ceramic, elaboration in glass
Size: between 5cm-15cm for each piece

The sculptures evoke different insights, where by each individual creates
its own interpretation. The black color could refer to mortality, the
transparancy to life and clarity. Yet both forms look fragile and
dangerous due to sharp points.






WATER & GLASS
2018

Assignment: translating the feeling of being underwater to glass
Material: glass
Size: between 10cm-20cm

A representation of the experience of being under water by portraying the depth
and stratification in combination with the fascination form air bubbles. This work
allows the viewer to enjoy an image that I experience on a daily base while practicing
for swimming competitions. Over the years, water has become an important part of my life.
